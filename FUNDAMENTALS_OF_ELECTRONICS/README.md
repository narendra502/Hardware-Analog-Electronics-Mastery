# 1. Origin of AC Power to House

Electricity is generated in power stations using:
- Thermal Power
- Hydro Power
- Wind Power
- Solar Power
- Nuclear Power

Generated AC power passes through:
1. Generator
2. Step-Up Transformer
3. Transmission Lines
4. Step-Down Transformer
5. Distribution Lines
6. Home Supply

In India:
- Home Supply = 230V AC
- Frequency = 50Hz

# 2. Atom Structure

Atom contains:
- Proton (+)
- Neutron (0)
- Electron (-)

Electrons move around nucleus.

Movement of electrons creates electricity.

---

# 3. Proton, Neutron, Electron

| Particle | Charge | Purpose |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Proton | Positive | Defines element |
| Neutron | Neutral | Stability |
| Electron | Negative | Current flow |

Electronics mainly depends on electron movement.

# 4. How Electricity is Generated

Electricity is generated when conductors cut magnetic field.

Main principle:
Faraday's Law of Electromagnetic Induction

Examples:
- Generator
- Alternator
- Dynamo

Mechanical energy converts into electrical energy.

## Aluminium

- Good conductivity

Used in:
- Wires

- Transformers

---

# 6. Why Circuit Needs Closed Loop

Current flows only when path is complete.

Open Circuit:
- No current flow

Closed Circuit:
- Current flows

Reason:
Electrons need complete return path to source.

# 8. Conductors, Insulators, Semiconductors

## Conductors
Allow current flow.
Examples:
- Copper
- Aluminium
- Silver

## Insulators
Block current flow.
Examples:
- Plastic
- Rubber
- Glass

## Semiconductors
Partially conduct electricity.
Examples:
- Silicon
- Germanium

Used in:
- Diodes
- Transistors
- ICs

---

# 9. Current Flow

Current = Flow of electrons

Unit:
Ampere (A)

Electron flow:
Negative → Positive

Conventional current:
Positive → Negative

---

# 10. Voltage Generation

Voltage is electrical pressure.

Generated by:
- Battery
- Generator
- Solar cell
- Power supply

Unit:
Volt (V)

Voltage pushes electrons through circuit.

# 13. Power Calculation

Formula:

P = V × I

Where:
- P = Power (Watt)
- V = Voltage
- I = Current

Energy Formula:

E = P × t
